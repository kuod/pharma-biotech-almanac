# Top 25 Drug Companies by Market Cap — 1950–2025

Seven decades of pharmaceutical dominance at 5-year intervals — from postwar antibiotics to GLP-1 blockbusters. Rankings, market caps, and key products across every era.

## Views

**Rankings** — Navigate any of the 16 five-year snapshots to see a ranked table of the top 25 companies, with market cap bars and key products for each.

**Heatmap** — All companies that ever appeared in a top-25 ranking, laid out across all 16 snapshots. Darker cells = higher valuation. Gray = not ranked that year. Click any year column header to re-sort; hover cells for details.

Coverage: 16 snapshots (1950–2025) · 6 countries (US, UK, Switzerland, Germany, France, Japan)

## Highlights

- **1950s** — Industry dominated by US companies emerging from the WWII antibiotic boom; European firms were embedded in chemical conglomerates
- **1960** — Oral contraceptive Enovid approved; Librium and Valium launched the tranquilizer era; Kefauver-Harris Amendments reshaped FDA approval after Thalidomide
- **2000** — Mega-merger wave formed GlaxoSmithKline, Novartis, AstraZeneca, and Aventis; Pfizer's acquisition of Warner-Lambert secured Lipitor, which became history's best-selling drug
- **2025** — GLP-1 revolution drove Eli Lilly from $130B (2020) to ~$750B, the largest five-year value creation in pharma history; Novo Nordisk's Ozempic/Wegovy created a $60B+ annual obesity market

## Usage

**Locally** — open `index.html` directly in any browser. No server or build step required.

**GitHub Pages** — enable Pages on the `main` branch root in repository Settings; `index.html` is served automatically.

## Data & Methodology

Pre-1990 figures are estimates compiled from historical industry analyses and annual reports. Post-1990 figures are reported market capitalizations. All values are in USD billions.
